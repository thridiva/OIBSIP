Scientific Contributions

Einstein's most famous equation, E=mc^2, is part of his theory of special relativity. His general theory of relativity, published in 1915, explained gravity as a curvature of spacetime caused by mass.

The photoelectric effect, for which he won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921, provided crucial evidence for the quantization of energy levels and the foundation of quantum mechanics.

Legacy

Albert Einstein's legacy extends beyond his scientific achievements. He was an advocate for civil rights, pacifism, and education. His ideas and philosophies continue to inspire and influence scientists and thinkers worldwide.
